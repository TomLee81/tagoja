Effective Date: May 1, 2026 | Last Updated: May 1, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how TAGOJA, a brand operated by SEOWOO LLC ("we," "our," or "us"), uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our platform.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit websites. They help websites remember information about your visit.

Types of Technologies We Use:

  • Cookies: Text files stored by your browser
  • Local Storage: Data stored in your browser
  • Session Storage: Temporary data cleared when you close the browser
  • Pixels/Beacons: Tiny images that track page views
  • SDKs: Code in mobile apps that collects usage data

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Make the platform work (essential functions)
  • Remember your preferences (language, settings)
  • Understand how you use our platform (analytics)
  • Show relevant content (personalization)
  • Improve security (fraud prevention)
  • Deliver advertising (with your consent)

4. How Long Cookies Last

Session Cookies

  • Deleted when you close your browser
  • Used for essential functions

Persistent Cookies

  • Remain until deleted or expired
  • Duration ranges from 24 hours to 2 years
  • Can be deleted manually

5. First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

First-Party Cookies

  • Set by TAGOJA directly
  • Used for core platform functionality
  • Fully under our control

Third-Party Cookies

  • Set by external services (analytics, ads)
  • Subject to their privacy policies
  • We select trusted partners only

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

6.1 Cookie Consent Manager

When you first visit TAGOJA, you'll see a cookie banner where you can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your preferences

Access settings anytime:

  • Click "Cookie Preferences" in the footer
  • Manage categories individually
  • Save your choices

6.2 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Receive alerts when cookies are set

How to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Google Chrome:

  1. Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  2. Choose your preference level
  3. Manage site-specific settings

Safari:

  1. Preferences → Privacy
  2. Choose "Block all cookies" or customize
  3. Manage website data

Firefox:

  1. Options → Privacy & Security
  2. Custom settings under Cookies and Site Data
  3. Manage exceptions

Microsoft Edge:

  1. Settings → Privacy, search, and services
  2. Manage and delete cookies
  3. Choose tracking prevention level

6.3 Mobile Devices

iOS: Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies

Android: Chrome → Settings → Site settings → Cookies

6.4 Opt-Out Tools

  • Google Ads: https://adssettings.google.com
  • Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/settings/?tab=ads
  • Network Advertising Initiative: https://optout.networkadvertising.org
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: https://optout.aboutads.info

7. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable all cookies:

  • ❌ You cannot log in
  • ❌ Shopping cart won't work
  • ❌ Preferences won't be saved
  • ❌ Security features may not function
  • ⚠️ Platform may not work properly

If you disable functional cookies:

  • ⚠️ Language resets to default
  • ⚠️ Settings don't persist
  • ⚠️ You may be asked to log in frequently
  • ✅ Platform still works

If you disable analytics cookies:

  • ✅ No impact on functionality
  • ℹ️ We can't improve based on your usage

If you disable marketing cookies:

  • ✅ No impact on functionality
  • ℹ️ Ads will be less relevant
  • ℹ️ Ads may appear more frequently

8. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers offer "Do Not Track" signals. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to DNT. We respect browser cookie settings and provide our own consent manager.

9. Cookies and Mobile Apps

Our mobile apps use:

  • App-based identifiers: Device ID, advertising ID

Manage mobile tracking:
