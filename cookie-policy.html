Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

What are cookies? Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit websites. They help us provide you with a better experience and enable essential functionality.

1. How TAGOJA Uses Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies (web beacons, pixels, local storage) to:

  • Keep you signed in
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our platform
  • Improve our services and features
  • Deliver personalized content and ads
  • Protect against fraud and abuse
  • Measure the effectiveness of our marketing

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

Purpose: Required for the platform to function properly. Cannot be disabled.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration

csrf_token Security protection against malicious attacks Session
auth_token Authenticates your identity 30 days

cookie_consent Stores your cookie preferences 1 year

2.2 Functional Cookies

Purpose: Enhance functionality and personalization.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration

Purpose: Help us understand how visitors use our platform.

4. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

4.1 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

4.2 Cookie Consent Tool

Use our cookie banner to customize your preferences:

  • Essential cookies: Always active (required for functionality)
  • Functional cookies: Optional (toggle on/off)
  • Analytics cookies: Optional (toggle on/off)
  • Advertising cookies: Optional (toggle on/off)

4.3 Opt-Out Links

  • Google Analytics: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
  • Facebook: Ad Settings in your Facebook account
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: http://optout.aboutads.info
  • Network Advertising Initiative: http://optout.networkadvertising.org

4.4 Do Not Track (DNT)

We respect Do Not Track signals. When DNT is enabled, we:

  • Disable non-essential tracking cookies
  • Turn off behavioral advertising
  • Limit data collection to essential functions only

5. Mobile App Tracking

Our mobile apps use similar technologies:

5.1 Device Identifiers

  • iOS: IDFA (Identifier for Advertisers)
  • Android: GAID (Google Advertising ID)

5.2 Managing Mobile Tracking

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking → Allow Apps to Request to Track
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization

We'll notify you of material changes via email or in-app notification.
